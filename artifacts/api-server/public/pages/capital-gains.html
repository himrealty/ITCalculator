Stocks & Equity Mutual Funds
Listed shares, equity MF, ELSS — Budget 2024 rates apply
Both Regimes ₹0 ▼
💡 For stocks and equity mutual funds, tax depends on how long you held them. Held more than 12 months = Long Term (12.5%). Held 12 months or less = Short Term (20%). These rates apply from 23 July 2024.

Property (Land / House / Flat)
Special indexation choice for pre-23 Jul 2024 purchases
Both Regimes ₹0 ▼
🏠 Property held more than 24 months = Long Term. If you bought it before 23 July 2024, you get to choose between 12.5% without indexation OR 20% with indexation — we'll show you which is better.

Gold & Jewellery
Physical gold, jewellery, gold ETF/funds
Both Regimes ₹0 ▼
💛 Gold held more than 24 months = Long Term at 12.5%. Held less = Short Term at slab rate. Note: Gold ETF listed on exchange qualifies as long term after 12 months (from Apr 2025 onwards).
📄
Debt Mutual Funds & Bonds
Always taxed at slab rate — no LTCG benefit
Both Regimes ₹0 ▼
⚠️ Debt mutual funds, bonds and debentures are always taxed at your income slab rate — regardless of how long you held them. There is no Long Term benefit here after the 2023 changes.
🏢
Unlisted Shares
Private company, startup shares — 24 month rule
Both Regimes ₹0 ▼
💡 Unlisted (private company) shares held more than 24 months = Long Term at 12.5%. Held less = Short Term at your slab rate.
💎
Other Capital Assets
Crypto treated as VDA (30%), art, patents, trademarks
Both Regimes ₹0 ▼
⚠️ Crypto / VDA (Virtual Digital Assets): Always taxed at flat 30% regardless of holding period. No deduction, no set-off against other losses.
Other assets (art, patents): 24 month rule applies — LTCG at 12.5%, STCG at slab rate.
