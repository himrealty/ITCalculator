House Rent Allowance (HRA)
Exemption on rent paid — Old Regime only
Old Regime Only ₹0 exempt ▼
✅ HRA exemption is calculated as the lowest of 3 things: (1) Actual HRA received, (2) 50% of Basic+DA if metro / 40% if non-metro, (3) Actual rent paid minus 10% of Basic+DA. The lowest of these 3 is exempt from tax.
⚠️ HRA exemption is not available in the New Regime. If you opt for New Regime, the full HRA is taxable.
Check your salary slip for "HRA" component

Leave Travel Allowance (LTA)
Travel expense reimbursement — Old Regime only
Old Regime Only ₹0 exempt ▼
💡 LTA covers travel costs when you go on leave within India. You can claim it twice in a block of 4 years. Only economy class air / AC train / bus fares are covered — no hotel or food bills.
⚠️ LTA exemption is only in the Old Regime. New Regime taxes the full amount.
Total LTA component in your annual salary

Perquisites (Perks)
Non-cash benefits from employer — taxable value
Both Regimes ₹0 ▼
💡 Perquisites are non-cash benefits your employer provides — like a company car, rent-free accommodation, or ESOPs. Your employer should tell you the taxable value of these in Form 16.
This should be mentioned in your Form 16 under "Perquisites". If unsure, check with your employer's payroll team.
Example: Company car perquisite value ₹1,800/month = ₹21,600/year
